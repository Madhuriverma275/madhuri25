Biography Page

Albert Einstein

Early Life

Albert Einstein was born in Ulm, Germany in 1879. From a young age, he showed great interest in science and mathematics. He studied physics at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic School in Zurich and graduated in 1900.

Achievements

Einstein is best known for his Theory of Relativity, which changed the way we understand time and space. In 1921, he received the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect. His work laid the foundation for many modern scientific breakthroughs.

Legacy

Einstein’s legacy continues to inspire scientists around the world. His curiosity, imagination, and determination set a remarkable example for generations to come. His famous equation, E = mc², remains one of the most iconic symbols of science.

My Daily Routine

Morning

I usually wake up around 6:30 AM. After brushing my teeth and doing a bit of stretching, I have a healthy breakfast. I enjoy starting my day early because it gives me enough time to plan things out and begin with a fresh mind. By 8 AM, I'm ready for school or work.

Afternoon

My afternoons are often busy and productive. I usually have lunch at around 1 PM and then get back to my tasks. Whether it’s attending classes or working on assignments, I try to stay focused and efficient. I also take a short walk or rest for a few minutes to recharge.

Evening

Evenings are my favorite time of the day. After finishing all my work, I spend time on my hobbies like painting or reading. I usually have dinner with my family around 8 PM. Before going to bed, I reflect on the day and sometimes write in my journal. It’s a peaceful end to a productive day.
